Spovis Haruna Yakubu


Born on 30th September, 1996, Yakubu Haruna, popularly known as Spovis, is often regarded as the founder of the Islamic Golden Eleven, Spovis Haruna Yakubu, was once a footballer who played for several teams at various levels.

Career


He featured in inter-school football competitions for St. Mary's Anglican Primary School and also New Atuabo Municipal Assembly Junior High School from 2007 to 2011
He was part of the 2011 team that won the maiden edition of Nunu Games within the Tarkwa Municipality
At Fiaseman Senior High School (FIASSEC), he joined the school team while in Year 1 (a rare thing given the fact that the school has an in-depth squad) but would have to wait for another year before making his official debut.
In 2014, he was a member of the FIASSEC team that won bronze at the Osagyefo Cup held in Nsein to commemorate the birthday of Ghana's first president, Osagyefo Dr. Kwame Nkrumah
He started his club football with Young Professionals FC under colleague and coach Farouk Adams, where he formed a lethal striking partnership with Austin Ekow Mercer. He was the team's all-time goalscorer.

After the team was dissolved in 2012, he joined his cousin Razak Adama's team, Young Chelsea FC. He had a short stint at the club due to admission into Senior High School education.
While in FIASSEC, he played for local side, Achiesu United FC from 2014 to 2017 where he was mostly used as a left back.

In 2021, he joined Herbal Boys FC where he currently plays for on a part time basis. They won the Tarkwa Basic Premier League cosecutively in 2022 and 2023.
